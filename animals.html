The Animals of Christmas Island

Manta Ray

Manta Rays possess the largest brains and brain-to-body ratio among all fish species. Research indicates that they may exhibit self-recognition in mirrors, a trait associated with advanced cognitive abilities. See them in our Snorkel Package!

Christmas Island Red Crab

Approximately 43.7 million adult Red Crabs inhabited Christmas Island exclusively. Their yearly mass migration to the ocean for breeding is deemed an "epic" spectacle. Simultaneously, millions surface, causing traffic disruptions and blanketing the terrain in a dense layer of crabs. See them in our Red Crab Migration Package!

Butterflyfish

Known for their vibrant color patterns, intricate shapes, and elegant swimming styles, the majority of Butterflyfish prefer solitary living or pair bonding. See them in our Snorkel Package!

Christmas Island Goshawk

The Christmas Island Goshawk is identified by its greyish-brown feathers with light barring, along with yellow legs and piercing golden eyes. These birds are commonly found in plateau and terrace forests. They exhibit a curious and tame nature, occasionally trailing behind hikers. See them in our Bird Watching Package!

Whale Shark

The Whale Shark, the largest living fish species, is a leisurely filter-feeder. It feeds exclusively on plankton and visits Christmas Island to consume the billions of larvae released during the annual Red Crab Migration. See them in our Snorkel Package!

Christmas Emerald Dove

This endangered subspecies of small, colorful pigeon is unique to Christmas Island, with an estimated population of around 2000 birds. Christmas Emerald Doves feed on fallen fruit on the ground and are often observed in pairs. See them in our Bird Watching Package!
